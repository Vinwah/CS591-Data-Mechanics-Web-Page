College Move-In Week

HOW DOES MOVING WEEK IMPACT THE QUALITY OF LIFE IN BOSTON?







  • Discover

Move in week is universally chaotic and a generally unpleasant experience for both, college students and local residents.

In this project, we attempt to isolate the most important factors that affect the residents' quality of life. With this information, we can model the problem as a relatively straightforward optimization problem with constraints.

311 Service Requests

Analyze Boston's 311 Service Requests records non-emergency city violations, such as improper trash disposal and illegal parking complaints, and the location of these incidents. With the understanding that the number of violations affects the quality of life on a given day in Boston, one of our aims became to find a way to serve requests in the most timely way. To make this solution concrete but also efficient, our team worked to display the minimum number of dispatchers required to respond to service within the time limit indicated by the 311 service request central.

From our analysis, we found that the period that has both the highest amount of requests and need the most dispatchers is the period associated with students moving in to universities in Boston, 28th of August to 6th of September. On the figures to the left, you can see a bar plot of both the number of service requests and number of required dispatchers, over a 10day period, as an average over all years in the same period. Other than the move-in period, the periods chosen are selected as baseline comparisons periods, selected from both during and outside of school semesters.

From these results we can see that the number of service requests increase with about 27% and minimum number of service request dispatchers increase with about 14%, from the second highest period, during move-in week.

DATASETS

Below are the datasets we used to complete our project.

CITY SCORE

This provides a consolidated metric that reflects residents' satisfaction with the city's efforts. We use the average of a collection of these values as an approximate indicator of how painful move in week really is.

311 SERVICE REQUESTS

This dataset contains over 400,000 detailed records of complaints and/or requests filed by residents. We sift through irrelevant requests by filtering for the type of service request (more details in the comments in the JS files), among others.




FIRE INCIDENT REPORTING

This dataset provides us with a rich history of fire incidents reported across the city of Boston, allowing us to glean trends, particularly during move in week.





SPATIAL DATASET OF COLLEGES AND UNIVERSITIES IN THE GREATER BOSTON AREA

This is used to construct 2d-index-capable queries on the other 4 datasets.
